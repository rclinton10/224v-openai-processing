District Engineer F. A. Hansen, in
company with Dr. E. S. Reedy left
Anchorage this morning for a trip of
several days over the Turnagain Arm
district.
A. W. Watson arrived in Anchor-
age on the Admiral Watson yester-
day afternoon and will spend the win-
ter in the city with his son Barney, of
the Admiral line.
Mrs. E. R. Davidson sailed for the
outside on the Admiral Watson and
will spend the winter in Spokane, re-
turning to Anchorage next spring.
J. H. Bartholf, interested in the
Willow Creek mining district, was an
outgoing passenger on the Admiral
Watson and will spend the winter in
the outside.

The dramatic club of the Alaska
Labor union are practicing for a vau-
deville performance that they expect
to stage at the Empress Monday
night, November 13th. The proceeds
of the evening will go towards build-

a new coat of white paint, which adds
to the looks of the place and is a ma-
terial assistance in the bowling.

Superintendent Eide and Ross
Kenny, of the Alaska Road Commis-
sion, returned yesterday from their
trip in the vicinity of Matanuska,
where they have been looking over
the wagon road situation for several
days and took passage on the Ad-
miral Watson for Seward.
W. L. Balch wires his partner, Ray
McDonald, that he has secured some
unusual attractions for the Harmony
theatre, among them being
Lew
Dockstader, Max Figman in "The
Hosier Schoolmaster," Marie Cahill in
"Judy Forgot," and the celebrated
Lena Cavalieri in "Mannin Lescout."
Mr. Balch is superintending the con-
struction of the new building for the
Bank of Alaska at Wrangell and in
his trip to Seattle in regard to ma-
terial was able to make a deal for the
above attractive productions.
Tom Howland, of Chitna, and a can-
didate for legislature on the democrat-
ic ticket arrived in Anchorage on the
Admiral Watson and will remain sev-
eral days in this vicinity in the inter-
est of his candidacy and the party
ticket. Mr. Howland is stopping at
the Crescent.
W. S. Perkins, head chef at the
Commission, will leave for the outside
on the U. S. A. T. Crook. Mr. Per-
kins has been called to New York on
some important financial matters rela-
tive to his father's large estate and
his many Anchorage friends wish him
success in the proceedings. Mr. Per-
kins has recently become interested in
the valuable Cassidy holdings in the
Willow creek district and will return
in the spring to devote his entire time
to the promotion of this property.
